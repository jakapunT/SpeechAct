# Speech Act Class Definitions

## Managing

**Short Description:**
Managing the classroom
(จัดการห้องเรียนที่ไม่เกี่ยวกับเนื้อหา)

**Definition:**
Managing speech acts refer to utterances used to build, maintain, or manage classroom interaction and teacher-student relationships. These utterances are not primarily about the lesson content. They may include greetings, jokes, encouragement, classroom instructions, or directives used to organize student behavior and participation.

**Example:**

> The teacher remarks, “I really enjoyed reading your homework reflections, and I’m looking forward to hearing more of your ideas in today’s discussion.”

---

## Facilitating

**Short Description:**
Asking questions and prompting students to respond or consider different points of view
(ถามคำถามให้นิสิตตอบ)

**Definition:**
Facilitating speech acts involve asking questions, prompting student participation, or encouraging students to consider different perspectives. These utterances usually seek responses from students, especially for formative learning purposes or to develop the lecture thematically.

**Example:**

> The teacher asks, “According to the CDC, what is the incubation period for this disease?”

---

## Using Metadiscourse

**Short Description:**
Utterances that organize, describe, or comment on the lecture discourse
(ประโยคในการเปลี่ยนหัวข้อ จัดระเบียบการสอน หรืออธิบายทิศทางของบทเรียน)

**Definition:**
Using metadiscourse refers to speech acts that organize, frame, repair, or comment on the lecture itself. These may include discourse markers such as “okay” or “right,” topic transitions, lecture outlines, signals of importance, rhetorical questions, or statements that clarify how the lesson is structured. Metadiscursive speech acts do not mainly provide new content, but instead guide how students should follow or interpret the lecture.

**Examples:**

> “As you’ve read for homework, this class will focus on three specific areas.”

> “Today, we are going to talk about heart disease.”

> “This detail is not as significant as the earlier part of this lecture.”

---

## Chronicling

**Short Description:**
Sequencing clinical stages, patient history, or events
(เรียงลำดับเหตุการณ์ต่าง ๆ ในอดีต จาก 1 → 2 → 3)

**Definition:**
Chronicling speech acts involve describing a patient’s clinical history, disease progression, or sequence of events. These utterances present information in a temporal, causal, or biological order, such as how symptoms developed, how a condition progressed, or how different stages of a case unfolded over time.

**Example:**

> The teacher describes how a patient’s symptoms evolved over the past three months.

---

## Explaining

**Short Description:**
Clarifying clinical facts, causes, classifications, or cause-effect relationships
(อธิบายข้อเท็จจริง รายละเอียด หรือเหตุผลว่าทำไมสิ่งหนึ่งจึงเกิดขึ้น)

**Definition:**
Explaining speech acts involve presenting factual information, clarifying concepts, describing mechanisms, classifying phenomena, or explaining cause-effect relationships. These utterances are used to help students understand what something is, how it works, or why it happens.

**Example:**

> The teacher explains how smoking can lead to lung damage and increase the risk of cancer.

---

## Exemplifying

**Short Description:**
Giving examples
(ยกตัวอย่างเพื่ออธิบายแนวคิด)

**Definition:**
Exemplifying speech acts involve providing examples to illustrate an idea, concept, condition, or application. The purpose of the example is to make an abstract or general concept more concrete and easier for students to understand.

**Example:**

> The teacher says, “For example, high temperature is one symptom we should look out for.”

---

## Directing

**Short Description:**
Giving procedural instructions or step-by-step guidance
(อธิบายขั้นตอนหรือวิธีการทำสิ่งใดสิ่งหนึ่ง)

**Definition:**
Directing speech acts involve instructing students on how to carry out a method, follow a protocol, perform a procedure, or understand a sequence of procedural actions. These utterances often describe ordered steps, such as first, second, and third, or provide guidance on what should be done in practice.

**Example:**

> The teacher instructs, “First, sanitize your hands, then put on sterile gloves before touching the wound.”

---

## Critiquing

**Short Description:**
Giving corrective feedback to students
(วิจารณ์คำตอบของนิสิตและชี้แจงจุดที่ตอบผิด)

**Definition:**
Critiquing speech acts involve correcting, challenging, or disagreeing with a student’s response. The teacher uses these utterances to point out errors, explain why an answer is incomplete or incorrect, and guide students toward a more accurate understanding.

**Example:**

> The teacher says, “Your diagnosis is not correct, because it is missing a very important factor.”

---

## Affirming

**Short Description:**
Agreeing with or confirming student responses
(ยืนยันว่าคำตอบของนิสิตถูกต้อง)

**Definition:**
Affirming speech acts involve agreeing with, confirming, or positively acknowledging a student’s response. These utterances indicate that the student’s answer, interpretation, or contribution is correct or acceptable.

**Example:**

> The teacher says, “Yes, you are correct.”
